About Me

Well, you know I'm Kiz and I'm a

But my Story began in Lagos, Nigeria, in a bilingual household speaking both English and Igbo. I've always loved to solve problems, taking apart my toys and reassembling them when I finished my homework. It wasn't until about 16, when my sister's best friend Nenne introduced me to programming that my love for it truly ignited. Shortly after, I came to America to study at the beautiful Bucknell University. With my passions for problem-solving, design, linguistics, and language learning, a computer science major was the clear choice. At Bucknell, I honed my programming skills and aligned them closely with my passions. I researched the acquisition of biases by large language models, developed beautiful games to be played by my peers, and stunning visualizers, to name a few. I am presently in the final stages of building mynigerianname, a project dear to my heart, and approximately 10% of Nigerians who have gone their whole life not knowing what their name means and the power it holds. I loved my time at Bucknell, but now I'm ready for the next big thing, working with you!

My Projects.

My Nigerian Name

My Nigerian name is not just an online dictionary for every Nigerian name but also a tight-knit community for people to share their naming stories and what their names personally mean to them.

Twitter Bot

My Nigerian Name's twitter bot to post hourly Name-Meaning pairs with the aim of fostering a sense of community by connecting with people individually. Similar to the share a coke with project.

THICC Lab

The Human in Computing and Cognition Lab exists to understand, and reimagine ways that Computing/Cognitive systems exist in the context of the Human, contextualizing sociocultural theory focusing on blackness and antiblackness.

Web Scraper

This is a webscraper built to scour the mammoths of MyIgboName and the Afamefuna project. It is designed to pull the Igbo Name-Meaning pairs that would power a large section of mynigerianname.

PvP Chess

This is a simple web chess app which was designed to not only be played by two opponents but which has the added functionality of a Player vs Computer mode. It learns how you play as you go using a genetic algorithm.

Jeopardy

As a Senior Design Project my wonderful team and I were tasked with creating a Jeopardy-style to be played by Bucknell computer science students in break rooms. It is currently owned by Bucknell Computer Science department.

Sorting Visualizer

Humans are visual creatures. These beautiful visualizers help us better innately understand how common sorting and pathfindng algorithms like Heapsort and Dijkstra's algorithm work through stunning visuaalizations.
